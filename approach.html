Three Pillars of Digital Transformation

Business leaders across all sectors are recognising digital transformation as the main force driving their businesses in the year ahead.

The challenge is to ensure the goal of the digital transformation programme remains focussed on where digital technologies and data analytics can deliver value. The goal is not to simply become digital! 

Common sources of value include:

  • Operational Value - Enhancing decision-making capabilities, increasing efficiencies or improving organisational agility.
  • Customer Value - Building awareness and brand interest, improving customer intimacy, providing a new service or business model.

In the face of such a disruptive influence, it can be tempting to launch a large scale digital re-engineering of the organisation. We prefer to see digital transformation augmenting your existing capability, combining the strengths of your employees skill with those of digital technologies to form a "bionic" company:

As your business transformation partner EMPEDATA will support your team, adding skills and experitise where requried, and working to develop your indiginous capability to ensure digital transformation is embedded in your company DNA. A key enabler of this approach is the adoption of Scrum. This agile approach to project management, ensuring the team has clarity of purpose, is empowered to make decisions and equiped with the capabiliies to deliver solutions drives high level performance, while demonstrating capability regularly and testing assumptions ensures high value outcomes are getting delivered!

Our approach to delivering digital transformation is built on three pillars. To find out more please Get in touch or click on the links below where you will find a brief summary and links to our favorite books on these topics for a more comprehensive explanation.

  • Strategise - Setting top level goals, establishing appropriate metrics while building the team and data environment capable of delivering success
  • Analyse - Performing exploratory analysis on existing data to provide actionable insights, discovering patterns, relationships and anomalies that can be used to deliver business value.
  • Productionise - Developing robust applications that manage the complete process of data capture, storage, processing and presentation to provide customers and internal business users the services they require.
