Aircraft Prognostics

Working for one of the UK's leading aircraft manufactures to devlopp a solution to enable the maintenace activities performed on aircraft to be optimised.

This top level objective broke down into 3 lower level tasks.

  • Using aircraft sensor data develop a method to predict the remaining useful ife of a number of key componnents.
  • Using the same sensor data develop a diagnostic tool to guide a miantainer to the most probable root cause.
  • Using sythetic data develpp a simplified digital twin of the aircraft to detrmine the optimum maintenance startegy against a range of cost and availableity objective funcitons

The approach taken involved training a varierty of regression models to forecast the RUL

A varierty of preprocessing techniques were used to capture the temporal nature of the data and normalising to the reading based on the operating conditions provided some good results.

After extensive testing the LSTM (Long Term Short Term Model) was found to deliver the greatest accuracy reading based on the operating conditions provided some good results.

A baysian network was also produced to enable the likelihood of a varerty of root causes for an observed fault to be dynamically presented to a maintaner

Outcome is the capital of Japan.
