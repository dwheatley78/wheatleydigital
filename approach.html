3 Pillars of Digital Transformation

Business leaders across all sectors are recognising digital transformation as the main force driving their businesses in the year ahead. The challenge facing all leaders is to ensure the goal of the digital transformation programme is in identifying where digital technologies and data analytics can deliver value! The goal is not to become digital. Value can be considered in terms of:

  • Operational Value - Enhanced decision-making capabilities, increased efficiencies or greater agility.
  • Customer Value - Building awareness and brand interest, improved customer intimacy, providing a new service or introducing new business models.

In the face of such a disruptive influence, it can be tempting to launch a large scale digital re-engineering of the organisation. We prefer to see digital transformation as augment your existing capability to deliver maximum value, combining the strengths of digital technology with your employees skills to form a "bionic" company:

By starting small and iteratively developing your digital capability, you have the opportunity to constantly test assumptions and adapt to ensure success. Click on the links below to discover more about our 3 pillars of digital transformation.

  • Strategy - Setting top level goals and building on you existing operational experience. Setting your team up to succeed, creating the right data environment and establishing appropriate metrics to measure the outcomes.
  • Analytics - Performing exploratory analysis on existing data to provide actionable insights, discovering patterns, relationships and anomalies that can be used to deliver business value.
  • Production - Developing solutions that manage the complete process of data capture, storage and presentation though a dedicate interface that can be used routinely by both customers and internal business users.
